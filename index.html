Animation:

Twelve Principles

In 1981, two Disney animators introduced the world to a set of principles that they, along with many others in their industry, had been applying towards animated productions for several decades. Said principles have been continually utilized and held with high regard due to their ability to provide an added sense of dynamism and liveliness towards animated characters, objects, etc. There are 12 in total:

1. Squash and Stretch, 2. Anticipation, 3. Staging, 4. Straight Ahead and Pose to Pose, 5. Follow Through and Overlapping Action, 6. Slow-In and Slow-Out, 7. Arc, 8. Secondary Action, 9. Timing, 10. Exaggeration, 11. Solid Drawings, and 12. Appeal
